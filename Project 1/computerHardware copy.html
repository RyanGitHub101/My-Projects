PC case

What does the case do?

  • Support and shield the components
  • Stores everything (CPU, GPU, motherboard, power supply unit, memory, hardrive)
  • Allows direct airflow to prevent hot air inside the case

Analogy:
Think of a skin. It protects other components and provide structure
in a well orangised way. You can also call a PC case "chassis"

Power supply unit

What does the power supply unit do?

  • Regulates power
  • Plugs outlet into the voltage switch
  • Converts Alternating current (AC) to direct current (DC)

Analogy:
Think of a heart (human body), it just blood pump blood to your whole body
so it can function. The power supply provides energy and power to the computer

Motherboard

What does the motherboard do?

  • Allows graphics card and processor to perform better
  • Communicate between components such as CPU, memory
  • Connects some hardware components which include CPU, RAM, RGB cooling fans, power supply and BIOS.

Analogy:
Think of a nervous system, the neurons send signals, or messages, all over
your body. The motherboard connects various parts of the computer and by
connecting something to each part something different happens.

CPU

What does CPU stand for?


• Central Processing Unit

What does the CPU do?

  • Runs computer programs
  • Runs low data storages
  • Create inputs and outputs
  • Peforms arithmetic and logic

Analogy:

The CPU is like the brain of the body. Our brain controls the human
body meanwhile the CPU controls the computer and most important
part of the computer. It's very important to know how a CPU works.

GPU

What does GPU stand for?


• Graphical Processing Unit

What does the GPU do?

  • Does alot of calculations in matter of milliseconds very quickly.
  • Carrys out small little task efficiently but sucks as complex instruction whereas CPU does that
  • Figures out what frame rate it should be depending on the graphics card
  • Figures out how to display images, pixels, textures, physics and more! in a three-dimensional world and two-dimensional world, also create images from binary data (1010101)

Analogy:

The GPU is like the human eye, our eyes connect to our brain and let us see
what's happening in the world around us. The GPU allows the computer to
display devices images around the screen with a resolution of 1920 x 1080p

RAM

What does RAM stand for

• Random Access Memory

What does the RAM do?

  • Stores small amount of data around 8GB to 10GB
  • Process data very fast depending on the RAM speed
  • Performs important task such as loading apps, browsing websites and
    high-performance play for gaming
  • Stores data on short term basis such as program or files that are being used repeatly in your computer system

Analogy:

The RAM is just like the pre-frontal lobe of the brain. The RAM plays a role
of short term memory, while the human pre-frontal brain plays a role of short
term memory

Hardrive

What does the Hard drive do?

  • Stores large amount of data around 128GB between 20TB
  • Stores all type of data such as photos, computer programs, video games, music and stores permanently inside the hard drive
  • Retrives data by using recyling bin at your OS, using a cloud based storage to delete data using the recyling bin
  • Process data slower than RAM because as you add more data to your hard drive, the drive mechanism must work harder to find the exact data you want, which slows down the retrieval of data

Analogy:

The hard drive is just like the hippocampus, the more you repeat something, soon it comes long term memory. The hard drive stores data long term about 3 to 5 years or potentially more! The reason is because of it's magnetic field to store information in 0s and 1s
